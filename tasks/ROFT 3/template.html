Instructions

We are conducting a research project to distinguish between human-written text and machine-generated text. We need to understand what clues humans use to determine which is which. 

Click the link below to be taken to the "Real or Fake Text" (ROFT) project website. Please log in with the username and password provided below. If you do not do this, we will not be able to see your annotations, and you will not get paid.

You will be shown a New York Times article one sentence at a time. Your task is to decide at what point (if at all) the text you see begins to be written by a computer. Don't be surprised if some excerpts are entirely human-written. In order for an annotation to be complete, you must determine the sentence where the given article switches from human to generated and give a comment about your reasoning. Your guess does not have to be correct, but it should show some thought.

You must complete 10 annotations to be paid -- if you do more than 10 annotations on the same account you will not be paid extra. If you would like to do more annotations, please accept the next HIT, which will assign you a new username and password.

The number of annotations you have completed so far will be displayed at the top of the page next to the "Real or Fake Text" header.
